

# FHA Mortgage Lending Guidelines — Quick Reference for Loan Officers

*Key rules that differ from Fannie Mae conventional lending*

---

## 1. INCOME QUALIFICATION

**Debt-to-Income (DTI) Ratios**
- **Front-end (housing) ratio:** 31% standard; up to 40% with compensating factors
- **Back-end (total debt) ratio:** 43% standard; up to 50% with compensating factors via AUS (TOTAL Scorecard "Approve/Eligible")
- **Manual underwrite:** 31% / 43% hard caps; can stretch to 37% / 47% with two compensating factors (e.g., reserves, residual income, minimal payment shock)
- *Compare to Fannie Mae: no front-end ratio requirement; back-end up to 50% via DU*

**Income Documentation**
- Most recent **2 years** of W-2s, tax returns, 1099s
- **30 days** of paystubs
- Self-employed: 2 years of personal AND business tax returns required; business must be operating at least 2 years
- Employment gaps >6 months in prior 2 years require LOE (Letter of Explanation)
- **Non-taxable income:** Can be grossed up by **25%** (vs. Fannie's 25% as well, but FHA is stricter on documentation of continuance — must show 3-year continuance likelihood)

---

## 2. CREDIT REQUIREMENTS

**Minimum FICO Scores**
- **580+** — Eligible for 96.5% LTV (3.5% down)
- **500–579** — Limited to 90% LTV (10% down)
- **Below 500** — Ineligible for FHA
- *Compare to Fannie Mae: generally 620 minimum for any approval*

**Manual Underwrite Triggers**
- No credit score / insufficient credit history
- TOTAL Scorecard returns "Refer" or "Refer with Caution"
- Manual UW minimum score: **580** for max financing; **640+** for DTI stretches above 31/43

**Waiting / Seasoning Periods**
| Event | FHA Waiting Period | Fannie Mae |
|---|---|---|
| Chapter 7 Bankruptcy | **2 years** from discharge | 4 years |
| Chapter 13 Bankruptcy | **1 year** into payback plan (court approval) or **2 years** from discharge | 2 years from discharge / 4 years from dismissal |
| Foreclosure | **3 years** from transfer of title | 7 years |
| Short Sale / Deed-in-Lieu | **3 years** | 4 years (short sale) / 4 years (DIL) |
| Mortgage Lates (housing event) | No specific seasoning but affects AUS | Similar |

**Collections & Charge-offs**
- **Medical collections:** Excluded from DTI — no payment required
- **Non-medical collections** with cumulative balance ≥ $2,000: Underwriter must use **5% of the outstanding balance** as a monthly liability OR borrower must pay off/set up payment plan
- *Compare to Fannie Mae: collections generally not included unless they affect creditworthiness per DU*

---

## 3. PROPERTY REQUIREMENTS

**Eligible Property Types**
- 1–4 unit primary residences ONLY
- HUD-approved condos (must be on the FHA Condo Approval list or get Single-Unit Approval)
- Manufactured homes (must be on permanent foundation, HUD-tagged, meet additional requirements)
- **NO investment properties. NO second homes.**
- *Compare to Fannie Mae: investment, second homes, and broader condo eligibility*

**Occupancy**
- **Owner-occupied primary residence only** — Borrower must occupy within **60 days** of closing
- At least one borrower must be an occupant

**LTV / Down Payment**
| Scenario | Max LTV |
|---|---|
| Purchase, FICO ≥ 580 | **96.5%** (3.5% down) |
| Purchase, FICO 500–579 | **90%** (10% down) |
| FHA Streamline Refinance | **97.75%** |
| FHA Cash-Out Refinance | **80%** |

**FHA Appraisal Requirements (vs. Conventional)**
FHA appraisals are **more stringent** — the appraiser acts as both a valuer and a property inspector:

- **Appraisal stays with the property** for **120 days** (transferable between lenders via FHA case number transfer). Conventional appraisals stay with the lender.
- **Health & safety deficiencies** must be repaired BEFORE closing:
  - Peeling/chipping paint on homes built **pre-1978** (lead paint) — must be scraped and repainted
  - Broken windows, missing handrails, exposed wiring
  - Roof must have **2+ years of remaining life**
  - Adequate heating for the climate; functional plumbing/electrical
  - No standing water in crawl space; proper drainage
  - Well and septic must meet local requirements (100 ft separation minimum)
- **Minimum property standards (MPS):** Property must be safe, sound, and secure
- **FHA requires a second appraisal** if the property was acquired by the seller within the past 91–180 days and the resale price is ≥ 100% above acquisition cost

## 4. LOAN LIMITS (2025 Figures)

- **Floor (low-cost areas):** $524,225 (single unit)
- **Ceiling (high-cost areas):** $1,209,750 (single unit)
- Set at **65% of the conforming loan limit** (floor) and **150% of the conforming limit** (ceiling)
- Limits vary by county — look up at [entp.hud.gov/idapp/html/hicostlook.cfm](https://entp.hud.gov/idapp/html/hicostlook.cfm)

| Units | Floor | Ceiling |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | $524,225 | $1,209,750 |
| 2 | $671,200 | $1,548,975 |
| 3 | $811,275 | $1,872,225 |
| 4 | $1,008,300 | $2,326,875 |

*Compare to Fannie Mae: 2025 conforming limit is $806,500 (1-unit, baseline); high-cost up to $1,209,750*

## 5. MORTGAGE INSURANCE — UFMIP & ANNUAL MIP

**This is the single biggest structural difference from conventional loans.**

**Upfront Mortgage Insurance Premium (UFMIP)**
- **1.75%** of the base loan amount, financed into the loan
- Example: $300,000 loan → $5,250 UFMIP → total loan = $305,250
- Refundable on a prorated basis if refinancing into another FHA loan within **3 years**

**Annual MIP (paid monthly)**

| Term | LTV | Annual MIP Rate |
|---|---|---|
| ≤ 15 years | ≤ 90% | 0.15% |
| ≤ 15 years | > 90% | 0.40% |
| > 15 years | ≤ 95% | 0.50% |
| > 15 years | > 95% | 0.55% |

*(Rates for loan amounts ≤ $726,200; jumbo FHA loans have slightly higher rates)*

**When Does MIP Drop Off?**
- **LTV > 90% at origination (most FHA loans):** MIP lasts for the **life of the loan** — it NEVER drops off. Borrower must refinance to conventional to eliminate MI.
- **LTV ≤ 90% at origination (10%+ down):** MIP drops off after **11 years**
- *Compare to Fannie Mae: PMI cancels automatically at 78% LTV based on original value; borrower-requested at 80%*

## 6. FHA-SPECIFIC RULES

**Anti-Flipping Rule (12 USC 203b)**
- Seller must have owned the property for **>90 days** before a purchase contract can be executed
- Days 91–180: If resale price is **100%+ above** the seller's acquisition price, a **second appraisal** is required (at lender's expense)
- Days 181–365: Lender discretion on second appraisal if price increase is suspicious
- *No equivalent rule in conventional lending*

**Identity of Interest (Related-Party Transactions)**
- Transactions between parties with a relationship (family, business associates) are limited to **85% LTV** (15% down)
- **Exceptions allowing 96.5% LTV:**
  - Family member purchasing another family member's primary residence as their own primary residence
  - Employee/employer relocation purchase
  - Tenant purchasing the unit they currently rent (must document 6-month rental history)

**Non-Occupant Co-Borrowers**
- FHA **allows** non-occupant co-borrowers (typically family members) — max LTV **96.5%** if the co-borrower is a family member
- Non-family non-occupant co-borrower: max **75% LTV**
- Co-borrower's income CAN be used for qualifying; co-borrower is fully liable
- *Compare to Fannie Mae: also allows non-occupant co-borrowers but limited to 95% LTV for 1-unit*

**Gift Funds**
- **100% of the down payment** can come from a gift — no minimum borrower contribution required
- Acceptable donors: family members, employers, labor unions, charitable organizations, government agencies
- **Gift letter required:** Must state amount, donor relationship, confirm no repayment expected
- **Paper trail required:** Donor's bank statement showing withdrawal + borrower's account showing deposit
- Gifts from interested parties (seller, realtor, builder) are **NOT** permitted as down payment
- *Compare to Fannie Mae: gifts allowed for primary residence but some programs require minimum 5% borrower own funds on certain occupancy/property types*

**Seller Concessions**
- Maximum **6%** of the sales price (covers closing costs, prepaids, discount points)
- *Compare to Fannie Mae: 3% if LTV > 95%; 6% if LTV 75.01–90%; 9% if LTV ≤ 75%*

**Down Payment Assistance (DPA)**
- FHA allows DPA from government entities, HUD-approved nonprofits, and employer-assistance programs
- DPA can cover the entire 3.5% minimum down payment
- Secondary financing (silent seconds) allowed from governmental entities
- DPA from **sellers or other interested parties is prohibited**

**Assumability**
- FHA loans are **assumable** with lender qualification of the new borrower (creditworthy buyer can take over the existing FHA loan at its current rate and terms)
- *Conventional loans are generally NOT assumable*

**FHA Amendatory Clause & Real Estate Certification**
- Required addendum: Buyer is not obligated to complete the purchase if the appraised value is less than the contract price
- Buyer must acknowledge they have inspected or waived inspection of the property
- *No equivalent required form in conventional lending*

---

## Key Takeaway Differences vs. Conventional (Fannie Mae)

| Feature | FHA | Fannie Mae Conventional |
|---|---|---|
| Min FICO | 500 (with 10% down) / 580 (3.5% down) | 620 |
| Min Down Payment | 3.5% | 3% (HomeReady/97) |
| MI Removal | Life of loan if <10% down | Auto-cancels at 78% LTV |
| UFMIP | 1.75% financed | None |
| Max Seller Concessions | 6% flat | 3–9% tiered by LTV |
| Property Condition | Strict MPR/MPS | Appraisal waiver possible |
| Bankruptcy Wait | 2 years (Ch. 7) | 4 years (Ch. 7) |
| Foreclosure Wait | 3 years | 7 years |
| Investment / 2nd Home | Not allowed | Allowed |
| Assumable | Yes | No |
| Anti-Flip Rule | 90-day minimum | None |
