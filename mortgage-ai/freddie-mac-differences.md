

# Key Differences: Fannie Mae vs. Freddie Mac Mortgage Guidelines

## 1. Affordable Products: HomeReady (Fannie) vs. Home Possible (Freddie)

| Area | Fannie Mae HomeReady | Freddie Mac Home Possible |
|------|---------------------|--------------------------|
| **Income Limit** | 80% of AMI (Area Median Income) — applies to ALL census tracts | 80% of AMI — but NO income limit in underserved (low-income) census tracts |
| **Minimum Borrower Contribution** | No minimum from borrower's own funds (1-unit, LTV ≤97%) | No minimum from borrower's own funds (1-unit, LTV ≤97%) — same |
| **Homeownership Education** | Required when ALL borrowers are first-time homebuyers (Framework) | Required for ALL purchase transactions where all borrowers are first-time buyers (CreditSmart) — each agency mandates its own platform |
| **Boarder/Rental Income** | Allows boarder income (documented 12-mo history) to qualify | Does NOT allow boarder income |
| **Non-occupant Co-borrower** | Permitted | NOT permitted on Home Possible |
| **Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU) Income** | Allows ADU rental income for qualifying | Not explicitly supported for Home Possible |

## 2. LTV / CLTV Differences (Standard & High-Balance)

| Scenario | Fannie Mae | Freddie Mac |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| **1-unit Purchase/Rate-Term Refi max LTV** | 97% (with HomeReady or 97% LTV program) | 97% (with Home Possible or HomeOne) |
| **2-unit Purchase max LTV** | 85% | 85% — same |
| **3-4 unit Purchase max LTV** | 75% | 80% (Freddie is MORE lenient on 3-4 units) |
| **Cash-out Refi 1-unit max LTV** | 80% | 80% — same |
| **Cash-out Refi 2-4 unit max LTV** | 75% (2-unit), 75% (3-4 unit) | 75% (2-unit), 75% (3-4 unit) — similar but check matrices |
| **Super Conforming / High-Balance max LTV** | Fannie uses "High-Balance" — max 95% LTV 1-unit purchase | Freddie uses "Super Conforming" — max 95% LTV 1-unit purchase; Freddie historically has slightly different CLTV caps on high-balance cash-outs |
| **Investment Property Purchase** | 85% (1-unit), 75% (2-4 unit) | 85% (1-unit), 75% (2-4 unit) — similar but Freddie caps at 1-unit for some DU/LP Accept paths |

## 3. Mortgage Insurance (MI) Differences

| Area | Fannie Mae | Freddie Mac |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| **MI Cancellation** | Standard HPA rules; borrower-initiated at 80% LTV, auto-terminates at 78% | Same federal HPA rules apply — no meaningful difference |
| **MI Coverage Requirements** | Uses its own coverage tables (varies by LTV, loan term, credit score) | Uses its OWN coverage tables — Freddie's required coverage percentages are SLIGHTLY DIFFERENT at certain LTV/credit score bands (e.g., Freddie may require 35% coverage at >95% LTV vs. Fannie's 30% for certain scores) |
| **Reduced MI Options** | HomeReady offers reduced MI coverage levels (as low as 25% at ≤97% LTV for ≥760 FICO) | Home Possible also offers reduced MI, but Freddie's reduced schedule differs slightly — generally competitive but not identical percentages |
| **LPMI (Lender-Paid)** | Permitted; lender negotiates rate with MI company | Permitted; same general concept but pricing differs by insurer |

## 4. DTI Differences

| Area | Fannie Mae | Freddie Mac |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| **Standard Max DTI** | 45% (manual underwrite); up to **50%** with DU Approve/Eligible with strong compensating factors | 45% (manual underwrite); up to **50%** with LP Accept — but Freddie has historically been slightly TIGHTER on DTI stretches above 45% |
| **AUS-Driven DTI** | DU can approve up to ~50% DTI routinely with good credit/reserves | LP (Loan Prospector) can also go to 50% but tends to require STRONGER compensating factors (higher credit score, more reserves) at the same DTI |
| **HomeReady Max DTI** | Up to 50% via DU | Home Possible up to 50% via LP — but layered risk treatment may differ |
| **Manual UW Max DTI** | 36% standard, 45% with compensating factors | 43% standard, up to 45% with compensating factors — Freddie is STRICTER on manual UW DTI |

## 5. Gift Fund Rule Differences

| Area | Fannie Mae | Freddie Mac |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| **Minimum Borrower Contribution (standard)** | 5% own funds required on 2-4 unit; NO minimum on 1-unit ≤80% LTV from borrower's own funds | 5% own funds required on 2-4 unit; For 1-unit >80% LTV, Freddie requires **at least 3% from borrower's own funds** on standard products (NOT Home Possible) |
| **Gift on 1-unit >80% LTV (non-affordable)** | 100% gift permitted — NO borrower own funds needed (1-unit, primary, any LTV) | Borrower must contribute **at least 3% own funds** on standard products if LTV >80% (1-unit). Gifts can cover the rest. This is a MAJOR difference. |
| **Gift Donors Allowed** | Relative, fiancé, domestic partner, church/nonprofit, employer | Same general list — but Freddie also explicitly allows gifts from **close friends** with a documented relationship (slightly broader) |
| **Gift of Equity** | Allowed on primary residence purchases from family | Allowed — same treatment |

**This is one of the most significant differences in practice.** On a standard (non-affordable) 1-unit purchase at 95% LTV, Fannie allows the entire 5% down to come from a gift, while Freddie requires at least 3% from the borrower's own funds.

## 6. Condo Project Review Differences

| Area | Fannie Mae | Freddie Mac |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| **Full Review** | Fannie uses "Full Review" with specific concentration limits (no more than 50% investor-owned for conventional) | Freddie uses "Full Review" but has **different investor concentration limits** — allows up to 50% as well but measures it differently in some cases |
| **Limited Review** | Available for LTVs ≤80% (attached) and ≤90% (detached) on established projects | Available with different LTV thresholds — Freddie's limited review LTV caps may differ by project type |
| **Project Eligibility (New Construction)** | Requires presale of 50% of units for new condo projects (can be lower with exceptions) | Freddie requires **70% presale** on new condo projects — STRICTER than Fannie |
| **Non-Warrantable Condos** | Not eligible (must meet project standards or use limited review) | Not eligible — same |
| **Condo Project Manager (CPM)** | Fannie's online condo tool for project vetting | Freddie does NOT have an equivalent portal; relies on lender rep & warrant |
| **Commercial Space** | Max 35% commercial/non-residential space | Max 25% commercial/non-residential space — Freddie is STRICTER |
| **Single-Entity Ownership** | No single entity can own more than 20% of units in project | No single entity can own more than **20%** — same threshold but Freddie applies it slightly differently for 2-4 unit projects |

## 7. Self-Employment Documentation Differences

| Area | Fannie Mae | Freddie Mac |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| **Tax Returns Required** | Most recent **1 year** if DU findings allow (2 years for complex/declining income) | Most recent **2 years** is the standard; LP may reduce to 1 year with strong findings, but Freddie defaults to 2 years more often |
| **Business Verification** | Verbal or written VOE from third party, OR business license, OR CPA letter, OR Fannie-approved verification methods | Similar — but Freddie explicitly accepts a **phone listing or directory listing** as standalone verification; Fannie typically wants more substantive evidence |
| **Declining Income Analysis** | Required when income trend is declining — underwriter must document why income is stable/likely to continue | Same concept, but Freddie provides MORE PRESCRIPTIVE guidance on how to calculate the declining income trend (specific averaging methodology) |
| **P&L Statements** | Year-to-date P&L required if >3 months into current year; audited P&L preferred but unaudited accepted | Similar requirement — but Freddie may require the P&L to be **signed by borrower** (not just prepared by CPA) in certain scenarios |
| **Business Asset Use for Down Payment** | Allowed if documented and business can sustain withdrawal | Allowed — but Freddie requires a **more specific liquidity analysis** of the business post-withdrawal |

## 8. Unique Freddie Mac Programs & Distinctions

| Program/Feature | Details |
|----------------|---------|
| **HomeOne** | Freddie's 97% LTV program with NO income limits (unlike Home Possible). Fannie's equivalent is the "97% LTV" option under standard guidelines — but Fannie ties it more closely to HomeReady or requires first-time homebuyer status. HomeOne is cleaner/simpler. |
| **Super Conforming** | Freddie uses the term "Super Conforming" for high-balance loans. Same concept as Fannie's "High-Balance" but Freddie's pricing adjustments (LLPAs) are structured differently — Freddie often has slightly better pricing on super conforming in certain credit/LTV bands. |
| **Refi Possible** | Freddie's no-cash-out refinance program for borrowers at or below 80% AMI. Fannie does NOT have a direct equivalent low-income refi product branded this way. |
| **CHOICERenovation** | Freddie's renovation loan product. Fannie's equivalent is HomeStyle Renovation. Key difference: CHOICERenovation allows the renovation to be completed by the borrower (sweat equity for up to $15K in some cases); HomeStyle generally requires licensed contractors for all work. |
| **CHOICEHome** | Freddie's program for factory-built homes (modular/manufactured). Broader eligibility for certain factory-built types compared to Fannie's standard manufactured housing guidelines. |
| **GreenCHOICE** | Freddie offers financing for energy-efficient improvements bundled into the mortgage. Fannie has a similar concept but Freddie's branding/program structure is more formalized. |
| **Asset & Income Modeler (AIM)** | Freddie's tool for streamlined income/asset analysis through LP. Fannie uses DU Validation Service (Day 1 Certainty) — different platforms with different data providers and waiver logic. |

## Summary of the Most Impactful Differences for Loan Officers

1. **Gift funds on standard products (>80% LTV):** Fannie allows 100% gift; Freddie requires 3% borrower own funds. This alone determines which agency to use for gift-heavy borrowers.

2. **Home Possible vs. HomeReady income limits:** Freddie waives the income cap in underserved tracts; Fannie applies 80% AMI everywhere.

3. **Manual underwrite DTI:** Freddie caps at 43% (45% with compensating factors); Fannie allows 45% more readily.

4. **Condo new construction presale:** Freddie requires 70% vs. Fannie's 50%. Freddie also caps commercial space at 25% vs. 35%.

5. **Self-employment tax returns:** Freddie defaults to 2 years; Fannie/DU more frequently allows 1 year.

6. **3-4 unit LTVs:** Freddie allows 80% LTV on purchases vs. Fannie's 75% — a meaningful advantage for small multi-family.

7. **Boarder income:** Only Fannie (HomeReady) allows it.

---

*Note: Guidelines change frequently. These differences reflect the general framework as of early 2026. Always verify against current Selling Guides (Fannie Mae Selling Guide and Freddie Mac Single-Family Seller/Servicer Guide) before making lending decisions.*
