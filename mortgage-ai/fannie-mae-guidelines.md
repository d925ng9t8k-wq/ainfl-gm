

# Fannie Mae Lending Guidelines — Quick Reference

*Compiled from standard Fannie Mae Selling Guide knowledge. Note: Fannie Mae updates its Selling Guide periodically. The figures below reflect guidelines current through early-to-mid 2025 (the latest in my training data). The 2026 conforming loan limits have not yet been announced as of my knowledge cutoff; I have noted where estimates or 2025 figures are used.*

---

## 1. INCOME QUALIFICATION

### DTI (Debt-to-Income) Ratio Limits

| Scenario | Maximum DTI |
|---|---|
| Standard (manually underwritten) | 36% (with no compensating factors) |
| Manually underwritten with compensating factors | Up to 45% |
| DU (Desktop Underwriter) Approve/Eligible | Up to 50% |
| DU with strong compensating factors (high reserves, low LTV, high credit score) | Up to 50% (DU will not approve above 50%) |

- The 50% DTI cap via DU is a hard ceiling. There is no Fannie Mae path above 50% DTI.
- "Total" DTI includes all minimum required payments on revolving debt, installment debt, housing expense (PITIA), and other recurring obligations (child support, alimony, etc.).

### Income Documentation Requirements

- **W-2 borrowers:** Most recent 30 days paystubs + most recent 2 years W-2s. If income is stable or increasing, 1 year of tax returns may suffice in DU; if declining or variable, 2 years of returns required.
- **Commission / bonus / overtime income:** Must be received for at least 12 months (with a 2-year history preferred) and be likely to continue. If less than 25% of total income, 1 year of documentation may suffice.
- **Variable income trending:** If trending downward, use the lower of the two-year average or the most recent year. If increasing, either the two-year average or the most recent 12 months can be used (lender discretion, DU will sometimes specify).

### Self-Employment Income Rules

- **Minimum history:** 2 years of self-employment required (with limited exceptions for borrowers with prior related experience).
- **Documentation:** 2 years of personal tax returns (1040) AND 2 years of business tax returns (1065, 1120, 1120S) if ownership is 25% or greater.
- **Year-to-date P&L:** A YTD profit-and-loss statement is required if more than a calendar quarter has passed since the end of the last fiscal year. If the P&L is not audited, a CPA-prepared or borrower-prepared statement is acceptable, but some lenders require CPA-prepared.
- **Income calculation:** Net self-employment income is used (not gross). Depreciation and certain non-cash expenses (depletion, amortization, meals/entertainment exclusion) can be added back. Business use of home deduction is added back. If the business shows a loss, the loss must be counted against the borrower's qualifying income.
- **Business liquidity:** If business funds are used for down payment/closing costs, the lender must confirm withdrawal will not adversely affect the business.

## 2. CREDIT REQUIREMENTS

### Minimum Credit Scores

| Loan Type | Minimum Representative Credit Score |
|---|---|
| Fixed-rate, LTV ≤ 75% | 620 |
| Fixed-rate, LTV > 75% | 620 |
| ARMs | 620 |
| General Fannie Mae minimum (all products) | **620** |
| HomeReady | 620 (with enhanced pricing at 680+) |
| High-balance / super conforming | 620 (though pricing adjustments are steep below 700) |

- The "representative" credit score for a single borrower is the middle of three scores (or lower of two). For multiple borrowers, the lowest representative score among all borrowers is used for pricing; however, DU uses the average of the two borrowers' representative scores for risk assessment (a change implemented in late 2023).

### Credit Score Tiers and Pricing (LLPA) Implications

Fannie Mae's Loan-Level Price Adjustments (LLPAs) are organized in a matrix by credit score and LTV. Key breakpoints:

| Credit Score Range | Relative Pricing Impact |
|---|---|
| 780+ | Best pricing; minimal LLPAs |
| 760–779 | Very favorable; small adjustments |
| 740–759 | Slightly higher adjustments |
| 720–739 | Moderate adjustments |
| 700–719 | Noticeable adjustments |
| 680–699 | Significant adjustments, especially at higher LTVs |
| 660–679 | Substantial adjustments |
| 640–659 | Heavy adjustments; many lenders impose overlays here |
| 620–639 | Maximum LLPAs; limited product eligibility with many lenders |

- Example magnitude: A borrower at 660 credit score / 95% LTV might face LLPAs totaling 2.75–3.25% in fee (translating to roughly 0.75–1.00% higher rate), while a 780+ / 60% LTV borrower might face near-zero LLPAs.
- First-time homebuyer LLPAs were restructured in May 2023; borrowers at or below 100% AMI receive LLPA waivers on certain products.

### Derogatory Credit Waiting Periods

| Event | Waiting Period (from discharge/completion date) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Chapter 7 Bankruptcy | **4 years** | From discharge date |
| Chapter 13 Bankruptcy | **2 years** from discharge; **4 years** from dismissal | Must show re-established credit |
| Foreclosure | **7 years** | From completion date (deed transfer, sheriff's sale, etc.) |
| Deed-in-Lieu of Foreclosure | **4 years** | From execution date |
| Short Sale / Pre-Foreclosure Sale | **4 years** | From completion date |
| Loan Modification (on mortgage) | No set waiting period | But payment history on the modified loan is scrutinized |
| Multiple Bankruptcies | **5 years** from most recent discharge | If more than one filing in past 7 years |

**Extenuating circumstances** (documented event beyond borrower's control — job loss due to employer closure, serious medical event, divorce, natural disaster): Waiting periods may be reduced by approximately half (e.g., Chapter 7 bankruptcy reduced from 4 years to 2 years; foreclosure from 7 years to 3 years). Must be fully documented and the event must be a one-time occurrence.

## 3. PROPERTY REQUIREMENTS

### Eligible Property Types

| Property Type | Eligible? | Key Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Single-family residence (SFR) | Yes | Detached or attached |
| PUD (Planned Unit Development) | Yes | HOA review generally not required for established PUDs |
| Condo (warrantable) | Yes | Must meet Fannie Mae condo project standards (full review, limited review, or PERS) |
| Condo (non-warrantable) | **No** | Does not meet project standards (too many investor-owned, single entity owns too many, etc.) |
| 2-unit property | Yes | Owner-occupied or investment |
| 3-unit property | Yes | Owner-occupied or investment |
| 4-unit property | Yes | Owner-occupied or investment |
| 5+ units | **No** | Commercial / multifamily — not eligible for conventional conforming |
| Manufactured housing (MH Advantage) | Yes | Must meet MH Advantage standards (permanent foundation, specific construction/design features) |
| Standard manufactured home | Yes | With restrictions: single-wide generally not eligible; must be on permanent foundation, titled as real property |
| Co-op | Yes | Limited to specific states/markets; special requirements apply |
| Condotel / hotel-condo | **No** | |
| Mixed-use (residential + commercial) | Limited | Property must be primarily residential; commercial use cannot exceed 25% of total area |

### Condo Project Standards (Key Thresholds)

- No single entity can own more than **10%** of total units in the project (20% for new projects with 21+ units under limited review).
- At least **50%** of units must be owner-occupied or second homes (for established projects).
- No more than **15%** of total HOA dues can be 60+ days delinquent.
- Commercial space cannot exceed **35%** of total project square footage (25% for 2–4 unit projects).
- Project must be complete (no active phasing or construction for full review eligibility, with limited exceptions).

### Occupancy Types

| Occupancy | Eligible? | LTV Restrictions |
|---|---|---|
| Primary Residence | Yes | Most permissive LTV/CLTV |
| Second Home | Yes | More restrictive; must be suitable for year-round use, some distance from primary, borrower must maintain exclusive control |
| Investment Property | Yes | Most restrictive LTV; higher LLPAs; higher reserve requirements |

### LTV Limits by Property Type and Occupancy

**Primary Residence:**

| Property Type | Purchase / Rate-Term Refi Max LTV | Cash-Out Refi Max LTV |
|---|---|---|
| 1-unit (SFR/condo/PUD) | **97%** (with restrictions*) | **80%** |
| 1-unit standard | **95%** | **80%** |
| 2-unit | **85%** | **75%** |
| 3–4 unit | **75%** | **75%** |
| Manufactured housing | **95%** (MH Advantage) / **95%** (standard MH, limited) | **65%** |

*97% LTV available for: HomeReady, or standard loans with at least one first-time homebuyer and income ≤ 100% AMI, or Fannie Mae's 97% LTV product. Requires fixed-rate, MI required above 80%.

**Second Home:**

| Property Type | Purchase / Rate-Term Refi Max LTV | Cash-Out Refi Max LTV |
|---|---|---|
| 1-unit | **90%** | **75%** |
| 2–4 unit | **Not eligible** | **Not eligible** |

**Investment Property:**

| Property Type | Purchase / Rate-Term Refi Max LTV | Cash-Out Refi Max LTV |
|---|---|---|
| 1-unit | **85%** | **75%** |
| 2–4 unit | **75%** | **70%** |

## 4. LOAN LIMITS

### 2025 Conforming Loan Limits (latest confirmed figures)

| Unit Count | Baseline Limit (most of U.S.) | High-Cost Area Ceiling |
|---|---|---|
| 1-unit | **$806,500** | **$1,209,750** |
| 2-unit | **$1,032,650** | **$1,548,975** |
| 3-unit | **$1,248,150** | **$1,872,225** |
| 4-unit | **$1,551,250** | **$2,326,875** |

- **2026 limits:** As of my knowledge cutoff (May 2025), the 2026 limits have not been announced. FHFA typically announces new limits in late November each year, based on the Q3-to-Q3 change in FHFA House Price Index. Given recent home price appreciation trends (mid-single-digit annual growth), the 2026 1-unit baseline could reasonably be in the range of $840,000–$860,000, but this is speculative. Use the 2025 figures until FHFA publishes 2026 numbers.

### High-Cost Areas (Examples)

| Area | 2025 1-Unit Limit |
|---|---|
| Los Angeles County, CA | $1,209,750 |
| San Francisco County, CA | $1,209,750 |
| New York City (5 boroughs) | $1,209,750 |
| Washington, D.C. metro | $1,209,750 |
| Honolulu, HI | $1,209,750 |
| Denver, CO | ~$816,500 (slightly above baseline) |
| Most of Texas, Midwest, Southeast | $806,500 (baseline) |

- Alaska, Hawaii, Guam, U.S. Virgin Islands have a baseline floor of **$1,209,750** for 1-unit properties (150% of baseline).
- "Super conforming" or "high balance" loans (above baseline but at or below the high-cost ceiling) carry additional LLPAs, typically 0.25–0.50%.

## 5. COMMON OVERLAYS AND EDGE CASES

### Gift Funds Rules

- **Primary residence:** Gift funds can cover **100%** of the down payment, closing costs, and reserves on a primary residence if the borrower contributes at least **3% from their own funds** for LTVs above 80% on 1-unit properties... actually, Fannie Mae does **not** require any borrower contribution from own funds for 1-unit primary residences. The entire down payment can come from gift funds (1-unit primary only).
- **2–4 unit primary residence:** Borrower must contribute at least **5%** from own funds.
- **Second home:** Borrower must contribute at least **5%** from own funds; remainder can be gifted.
- **Investment property:** Gift funds are **not allowed**.
- **Acceptable gift donors:** Relative (by blood, marriage, adoption, legal guardianship), fiance/fiancee, or domestic partner. Gifts from interested parties (seller, real estate agent, builder) are **not** gifts — they are interested party contributions (IPCs) and are subject to separate limits.
- **Gift letter:** Must include donor name, donor relationship, dollar amount, property address, statement that no repayment is required. Must also document transfer of funds (bank statements showing withdrawal from donor and deposit to borrower, or wire confirmation).
- **Gift of equity:** Allowed on primary residence and second home purchases between family members. Treated similarly to cash gifts.

### Interested Party Contribution (IPC) Limits

| LTV | Maximum IPC (as % of sales price or appraised value, whichever is less) |
|---|---|
| > 90% | 3% |
| 75.01%–90% | 6% |
| ≤ 75% | 9% |
| Investment property (any LTV) | 2% |

### Reserves Requirements

"Reserves" = months of PITIA (principal, interest, taxes, insurance, HOA) the borrower has in liquid/semi-liquid assets after closing.

| Scenario | Minimum Reserves |
|---|---|
| 1-unit primary, ≤ 80% LTV, strong credit | 0 months (DU may not require any) |
| 1-unit primary, > 80% LTV | 0–2 months (DU determines) |
| 2-unit primary | 2–6 months |
| 3–4 unit primary | 6 months |
| Second home | 2–6 months (DU determines) |
| Investment property (1-unit) | 6 months |
| Investment property (2–4 unit) | 6 months |
| Multiple financed properties (5–10) | 6 months PITIA on subject + 2% of aggregate UPB of all other financed properties |
| High-balance loans | 0–6 months (DU determines; often higher) |

**Acceptable reserve sources:** Checking/savings, investment accounts (stocks, bonds, mutual funds — use 70% of value for retirement accounts to account for penalties/taxes), vested portion of retirement accounts, cash value of life insurance, gift funds (only if already in borrower's account and seasoned or documented per gift rules).

### Non-Occupant Co-Borrower Rules

- Allowed on **primary residence** only.
- The non-occupant co-borrower's income can be used to qualify, and their debts must be included in DTI.
- **LTV limit:** With a non-occupant co-borrower, maximum LTV is **95%** on a 1-unit primary (cannot use 97% LTV programs with a non-occupant co-borrower unless it is a HomeReady loan and the co-borrower is a family member).
- **HomeReady exception:** HomeReady allows non-occupant co-borrowers at up to 95% LTV without the co-borrower being related.
- **3–4 unit properties:** Non-occupant co-borrowers are allowed but LTV is capped at **75%**.
- If the occupant borrower has no credit score, the non-occupant co-borrower's score cannot be used as the representative score.

### Temporary Buydown Rules

| Buydown Type | Structure | Key Rules |
|---|---|---|
| 3-2-1 Buydown | Rate reduced by 3% year 1, 2% year 2, 1% year 3 | Borrower must qualify at the **note rate** (full undiscounted rate), NOT the bought-down rate |
| 2-1 Buydown | Rate reduced by 2% year 1, 1% year 2 | Same — qualify at note rate |
| 1-0 Buydown | Rate reduced by 1% year 1 | Same — qualify at note rate |
| 1-1 Buydown | Rate reduced by 1% years 1 and 2 | Same — qualify at note rate |

- **Fixed-rate loans only** — temporary buydowns are not permitted on ARMs.
- **Primary residence and second home only** — temporary buydowns are not allowed on investment properties.
- **Buydown funds:** Typically deposited into an escrow account by the seller, builder, or lender. These funds are considered an IPC and count toward IPC limits.
- The buydown subsidy (the difference between the bought-down payment and the full payment) is held in an escrow account and used to supplement the borrower's payment during the buydown period.
- If the borrower refinances or sells during the buydown period, unused buydown funds are typically applied to the loan balance.

### Additional Common Edge Cases

**Number of Financed Properties:**
- Fannie Mae allows a borrower to have up to **10 financed properties** (including the subject property).
- Properties 5–10 have additional requirements: minimum **720** credit score (for 7–10 properties; 620 for 5–6), **25%** down on investment SFRs (for 7–10 properties), and 6 months reserves on all properties.

**Asset Depletion / Asset Dissipation Income:**
- Eligible liquid assets (after subtracting funds used for down payment, closing costs, and required reserves) can be divided by the remaining loan term (in months) to create a "monthly income" stream. Typically, 70% of retirement assets and 100% of non-retirement liquid assets are used. This is useful for retirees or high-net-worth borrowers with limited traditional income.

**Rental Income from Subject Property:**
- For 2–4 unit primary residences, 75% of gross rental income (from units the borrower will not occupy) can be used to offset the PITIA. Requires a lease or appraiser's estimate of market rent (Form 1007 / comparable rent schedule).
- For investment properties, same 75% factor applies to gross rents.

**Student Loans:**
- If the monthly payment is reported on the credit report, use that amount.
- If the payment is $0 (income-driven repayment showing $0), use the greater of: 1% of the outstanding balance, or the actual documented payment. (DU will use 0.5% of the outstanding balance if the payment reported is $0.)

**Financed MI (Mortgage Insurance):**
- MI premiums can be financed into the loan amount, but the total loan amount (including financed MI) cannot exceed the conforming loan limit.

---

*This reference reflects Fannie Mae Selling Guide standards. Individual lenders frequently impose "overlays" — stricter requirements layered on top of Fannie Mae minimums (e.g., minimum 640 or 660 credit score, maximum 45% DTI even with DU approval, no non-warrantable condos, etc.). Always verify against the specific lender's guidelines and the most current version of the Fannie Mae Selling Guide at fanniemae.com.*
