Dynasty Buy / Sell / Hold After 2026 NFL Draft Round 1

Round 1 is done. Landing spots are confirmed. Here are the fast-fire dynasty takes on every player whose value moved tonight — rookies who soared, veterans who got buried, and the windows to trade right now.

How These Verdicts Work

BUY = value went up tonight, target them in trades now. SELL = value went down, move them before the market adjusts. HOLD = landing spot was neutral, no rush to move. These are dynasty-specific — not redraft.

+ BUY — Value Went Up Tonight
Ashton Jeanty — RB, Carolina Panthers
Pick 8 • Boise State • 6'0" / 215 lbs
The premier running back in this draft class lands in the single best backfield vacancy in the NFL. Carolina has no established starter, a run-first coaching staff, and the cap room to build around him. The dynasty community will panic-buy within 24 hours — get ahead of it. If you can acquire Jeanty for 1.01–1.03 in rookie drafts, you win the off-season.
Travis Hunter — WR, Cleveland Browns
Pick 2 • Colorado • 6'1" / 185 lbs
Hunter lands in one of the emptiest WR rooms in football. Cleveland will build the passing game around him from snap one. His two-way ability means he will be on the field constantly, and his route running and hands translate immediately to the next level. Buy now before rookie drafts drive his price to 1.01.
Tyler Warren — TE, New Orleans Saints
Pick 9 • Penn State • 6'6" / 258 lbs
Warren's landing spot could not be more favorable — thin receiving corps, a QB who leans on tight ends, and a creative offensive coordinator. In TE premium formats he is an immediate starter and a top-5 dynasty TE within two seasons. If you can get him for anything less than 1.04 in rookie drafts, do it without hesitation.
Tetairoa McMillan — WR, Las Vegas Raiders
Pick 6 • Arizona • 6'4" / 219 lbs
Las Vegas throws the ball, has no entrenched WR1, and runs a fast-paced offense that generates volume. McMillan's size is a weapon in a division full of physical corners. His floor is WR2 in Year 1 and his ceiling is a genuine WR1. Buy at late first-round rookie pick prices.
Cam Ward — QB, Tennessee Titans
Pick 1 • Miami (FL) • 6'2" / 221 lbs
In superflex leagues, Ward goes number one overall in rookie drafts. He is the clear franchise quarterback in Tennessee with a front office committed to building around him. His processing and arm talent are legitimate — this is not a situation where you stash and wait. He starts Week 1 and his dynasty trajectory is top-5 QB within two years.

Jeanty's arrival makes Carolina's current backfield entirely expendable. If you own Sanders or Hubbard in dynasty, sell them now — tonight — before the market processes Jeanty's landing spot. Neither player survives in a meaningful role once Jeanty takes over as the feature back.

Cleveland Browns WR • Dynasty veteran
Jeudy's target share just got carved in half. Hunter will immediately take over as the primary option and eat into any volume Jeudy was accumulating as a nominal WR1. In dynasty, Jeudy is a sell unless your trade partner is asleep — get a mid-round pick and move on.
Shedeur Sanders Dynasty ADPs (if owned pre-draft in top 3)
New York Giants QB • Draft capital consideration

= HOLD — No Panic, Situation Unchanged
Caleb Williams — QB, Chicago Bears
Pick 10 (Kelvin Banks) • Bears QB dynasty hold
Chicago drafting Banks at left tackle was the best possible outcome for Williams dynasty holders. A locked-in blind-side protector is exactly what Williams needed. Do not sell — his dynasty value improved marginally with this pick. Hold through training camp.
Malik Nabers — WR, New York Giants
Giants WR1 • Dynasty hold
Sanders arriving in New York is a long-term positive for Nabers — the Giants now have a legitimate franchise quarterback throwing to him. Short-term, nothing changes: Nabers is still WR1 on the depth chart. Hold. His value goes up if Sanders develops into what his college tape suggests.
Will Levis — QB, Tennessee Titans
Titans QB • Released or depth chart buried
Ward's arrival effectively ends Levis's tenure in Tennessee. If you own Levis in a deep dynasty league, he has minimal value as a backup-turned-starter-if-Ward-struggles. Hold if the cost-basis is zero. Drop if roster spots are tight.

The Three Moves to Make Right Now

Move 1: Acquire Jeanty at any price in the 1.01-1.04 range. His landing spot is the cleanest bell-cow situation in this class. Every day you wait, the price goes up. The dynasty community moves fast and Jeanty in Carolina will be the consensus 1.01 by morning.

Move 2: Trade away Panthers incumbent backs tonight. Sanders and Hubbard owners are holding a rapidly depreciating asset. The window to sell is the next 12 hours before the market fully adjusts. Move them for anything useful.

Move 3: Lock up Tyler Warren in TE premium leagues. The TE scarcity premium is real and Warren's landing spot accelerates his timeline. In premium formats, he is a top-5 TE immediately. His pre-draft ADP will not reflect tonight's news until tomorrow morning — trade now.

Remember

Rounds 2-7 happen Friday and Saturday. Do not overreact to Round 1 alone. Depth at every position gets added over the next two days and some of the best dynasty value in this class will come in the second and third rounds. Stay patient on players who fell — there is a reason they fell.
