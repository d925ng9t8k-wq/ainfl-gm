The Problem

Children Are Using AI Unsupervised.
The Consequences Are Real.

73 million children in the US. No guardrails between them and the most powerful language models ever built. Schools are banning AI. Parents are in the dark. And the worst-case scenarios are already happening.

⚠

Children Are Dying

Character.AI has been linked to the suicides of a 14-year-old in Florida and a 13-year-old in Colorado. Multiple families across Florida, Texas, Colorado, and New York have filed wrongful death lawsuits. Character.AI and Google agreed to mediate settlements in January 2026.

Sources: CNN, NBC News, Bloomberg Law
🔍

Parents Are Blind

70% of children use AI tools, but only 37% of parents are even aware. 49% of parents have never spoken to their child about generative AI. There is a massive gap between what kids are doing and what parents know.

Sources: Pew Research Center, Feb 2026; Common Sense Media
🚫

Schools Are Banning, Not Solving

83% of parents say schools have not addressed AI usage. 8 in 10 parents want more guardrails on AI for their children. Kentucky became the first state to sue an AI company for preying on children (Jan 2026).

Sources: EdWeek Survey, Feb 2026; Kentucky AG Office
💰

AI Companies Won't Self-Regulate

Character.AI only banned under-18 open-ended chats after lawsuits and deaths — too little, too late. Lawsuits allege chatbots engaged in sexually explicit conversations with children as young as 9. The industry is reactive, not protective.

Sources: NBC News, Torhoerman Law, 2025-2026
