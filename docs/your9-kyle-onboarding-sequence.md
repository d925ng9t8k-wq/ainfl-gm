## 3. Success Checklist — What "Working" Looks Like for a Mortgage Branch Manager

This is what Kyle should be experiencing at each milestone. The language here is written for Kyle to read and self-evaluate — not internal ops metrics (those are in the go-live doc). This is his frame.

---

### At 24 Hours

Your CEO has responded to every message you sent. Response time has been under 30 seconds for normal requests. If you sent a draft request, you received a draft. If you sent a research question, you received a structured answer — not a summary from memory.

Your dashboard is live and showing activity. The task pipeline has entries. Your velocity score has moved off zero.

You have not had to explain what a lock desk is, what pull-through means, or why a 45-day close matters. Your CEO already knows.

### At 7 Days

Your CEO has sent you 7 morning briefings without a miss. Each one referenced something specific from the day before — not a generic template.

At least one referral partner or borrower received a message that you approved but did not write. That communication represented your voice accurately.

At least once this week, your CEO flagged something you had not asked about — a follow-up that was overdue, a lock window that was approaching, a pipeline item that had gone quiet. It found that on its own.

You have not had to repeat yourself. Your CEO remembers the context from Day 1.

### At 30 Days

You can name one specific thing your CEO handles that you used to do yourself. Not a category. A specific recurring task that is now off your plate.

At least one referral relationship in your book has been meaningfully touched — a re-engagement message, a follow-up after a referral, a proactive rate update with context. That outreach ran through your CEO with your approval.

Your pipeline visibility has changed. You are asking your CEO questions you used to log into Encompass to answer. The answer comes back to you in plain English with a recommended action.

You have pushed back on your CEO at least once — disagreed with a draft, redirected a priority, corrected an assumption. That is a sign you are engaged and your CEO is adapting.

You have not had to contact support for a technical issue. Everything worked.

## 4. Quick-Start Actions — Five Things to Ask Your AI CEO on Day One

These are high-value, immediate demonstrations of what the team can do. Each one is something Kyle would normally spend 20-45 minutes on himself.

---

### Quick-Start 1 — Draft a Borrower Follow-Up

Say: "I have a borrower who went quiet after I sent their pre-approval letter. It's been 12 days. They were shopping for a $420,000 home in Anderson Township, dual income, first-time buyers. Draft a follow-up that checks in without feeling pushy."

What your CEO does: The Voice agent writes a warm re-engagement message calibrated for first-time buyers who may be overwhelmed by the process. It acknowledges the silence without making it awkward. It reopens the conversation and gives the borrower an easy entry point. You approve before it goes anywhere.

Why this matters: Most borrowers who go quiet after pre-approval are not lost. They are overwhelmed. The right message at the right time recovers the relationship. The wrong message confirms they should keep shopping. Your CEO knows the difference.

---

### Quick-Start 2 — Research a Competitor's Positioning

Say: "What are CrossCountry Mortgage and Fifth Third doing in the Cincinnati market right now? What are they promoting, and where are they showing up in front of agents?"

What your CEO does: The Mind agent pulls current positioning, publicly available rate promotions, review activity, and any recent local press or marketing moves. It delivers a structured competitive brief — not a list of links, but an actual summary of what they're doing and where you have an edge.

Why this matters: Knowing where the competition is loud helps you decide where to be present. You can't outspend them on brand, but you can out-relationship them. Your CEO tells you where to focus.

---

### Quick-Start 3 — Build a Compliance Calendar Snapshot

Say: "I have [X] loans in process. Here are the basic scenarios and estimated close dates. What compliance and timing deadlines should I be watching in the next 30 days?"

What your CEO does: Based on the transaction details you share, the Executor builds a plain-English watch list — TRID timing windows, lock expiration dates, conditional approval deadlines, and disclosure requirements that have date-sensitive consequences. It surfaces anything that needs a decision before it becomes a problem.

Why this matters: Compliance timing failures are not malicious — they happen when everyone is busy and no one is watching the calendar. Your CEO watches the calendar.

---

### Quick-Start 4 — Write a Referral Partner Outreach Message

Say: "I have a real estate agent I've worked with for three years. We closed four deals together last year and I haven't had a real conversation with her since January. Her name is [Name], she works at [Brokerage], and she's heavy in the Hyde Park/O'Bryonville market. Write me something to reconnect."

What your CEO does: The Voice agent drafts a genuine reconnection message that references the relationship history, is specific to her market focus, and opens a door without asking for anything. It does not sound like a cold email. It sounds like someone who knows her picked up the phone.

Why this matters: Referral relationships decay quietly. Most producing BMs know this. The problem is not intention — it's time. Your CEO removes the time obstacle so the relationship work actually happens.

---

### Quick-Start 5 — Summarize an Active Pipeline Deal

Say: "Walk me through what questions I should be asking my processor right now on a purchase loan at [stage]. Borrower is [brief profile], property is [basic info], and the issue is [what's stalled or unclear]."

What your CEO does: Pulls on its knowledge of the mortgage process to give you a structured set of questions and a checklist of what should have happened at this stage versus what you've described. It identifies the likely bottleneck and suggests the most efficient path to resolution — whether that's a processor call, a conditions response, or a borrower document request.

Why this matters: You already know this. But when you're running between calls and managing five other active files, having someone organize the question and frame the answer saves 20 minutes per deal.

## 5. Support Escalation — Who to Contact If Something Isn't Working

Kyle's support path is designed to require as little from him as possible. Most issues never leave the Telegram conversation.

---

### Step 1 — Ask Your CEO (Covers 95% of Situations)

If something feels wrong, feels off, or is not doing what you expected — start with your CEO. Send a message describing what happened and what you expected instead.

Your CEO will either fix it on the spot, explain what happened, or escalate internally without you having to do anything else.

Examples:
- "You drafted that email too formally. Make it sound more like how I actually talk."
- "I sent you a task yesterday and I don't see any response in the pipeline. What happened?"
- "The dashboard isn't showing the activity from our conversation this morning."

You do not need to file a ticket. You do not need to use a form. You just talk to your CEO.

---

### Step 2 — Technical Issue Your CEO Cannot Resolve (60-Minute SLA)

If your CEO is not responding at all, if the dashboard is offline, or if the Telegram bot is not delivering messages, something technical has gone wrong at the hub level.

Contact: Your9 support at the address provided in your welcome email.

What to include: What you were doing when it stopped working. A screenshot of any error message if you have one. What time it happened.

Response time: Within 60 minutes during business hours (8 AM to 6 PM Eastern, Monday through Friday).

You will not be asked to troubleshoot anything yourself. The support team handles it. You just need to flag it.

---

### Step 3 — Active Service Outage (15-Minute Response)

If your hub has been offline for more than 15 minutes and the support channel has not responded, your issue has been escalated to 9 directly. You will receive a status update within 15 minutes.

What this triggers internally: 9 triages the outage, determines whether the issue requires a code-level fix (handled by Tee), a credential re-provisioning (handled by 9 directly), or a hub restart. Kyle receives a plain-English status message as soon as the diagnosis is complete — before the fix is done, not after.

---

### What Kyle Should Never Have to Do

- Restart anything himself
- Access any configuration files or environment settings
- Explain his business context more than once (his CEO carries it forward)
- Wait more than 60 minutes for a support response during business hours
- Use a ticketing system or form to report an issue

If any of those things happen, it is a support failure on the 9 Enterprises side — not a Kyle problem.
