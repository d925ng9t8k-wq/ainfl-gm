01 — Evaluation Method

Scoring Framework

Each product is evaluated on six weighted criteria. Scores run 1–10. Weighted average determines strategic rank. Framework was agreed upon by 9 and Ara prior to individual scoring.

01
Market Viability
15% weight — Does a real, accessible market exist today?
02
Competitive Uniqueness
20% weight — Is there a defensible, differentiated position?
03
Ecosystem Synergy
20% weight — Does it strengthen or leverage other 9E products?
04
Likelihood of Success
15% weight — Given current team and resources, can it win?
05
Revenue Potential
15% weight — What is the realistic ARR ceiling by Year 3?
06
Mission Alignment
15% weight — Does it advance the 9E core mission?

04 — Strategic Synthesis

Key Insights & Decisions

Five conclusions that must drive the next 90 days of resource allocation.

The Platform IS the Strategy
9E Solution scored #1 because it is not a product — it is the container every other product lives in. Resource it first. Build it right. Every dollar in the platform multiplies the value of every product below it.
AI Underwriter is the Revenue Engine
Highest individual revenue ceiling in the entire portfolio. $15M ARR Y3 is not a stretch — it is a baseline if Rapid Mortgage is the anchor client. One signed enterprise deal funds the next 12 months of development across all products.
Pepper is the Soul — Not Optional
Consumer trust is built through Pepper. Every B2B client who encounters 9E will look for the human entry point. Pepper is that entry point. She is ranked #3 not because she is less important — but because Platform and Underwriter are more urgent.
Bottom 3 Need Hard Decisions Now
Chaperone, Hitchhiker's Guide, and Shop9 (rescoped) are consuming strategic attention without contributing proportional value. Shelving is not failure — it is capital discipline. Announce the decision, document the rationale, move on. Revisit at $5M ARR.
The Real Moat Is Integration
Any single 9E product is copyable by a well-funded competitor. The ecosystem is not. When a client uses AI Underwriter through the 9E Platform, onboarded by Pepper, supported by Trader9's capital — no single-product competitor can replicate that. Build integration deliberately.
Strategic Conclusion — Ara & 9
Products feeding each other is the real competitive moat.
9 Enterprises is not building a product company. It is building an operating system for businesses that happens to manifest as products. The competitive advantage is not any individual feature — it is the compounding network effect of every product making every other product stronger. This is the insight that separates 9E from every SaaS startup in the market. Protect it. Sequence around it. Never let short-term revenue pressure cause the team to build standalone islands that do not connect back to the platform.
