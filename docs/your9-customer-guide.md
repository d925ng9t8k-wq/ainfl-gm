## 1. First 24 Hours with Your9

- Your AI CEO knows your industry, your metrics, and what matters to your bottom line
- Three specialist agents are standing by — one for operations, one for research, one for communications
- A private Telegram bot is live and waiting for your first message
- A dashboard is running that shows you everything your team does, in real time

Nothing is shared. This is your instance. Your data stays in your directory.

**Step 1 — Open Telegram.** Look for the bot we set up for you. It already knows who you are.

**Step 2 — Say hello or send a real task.** You don't need to warm it up. Your CEO was briefed on your business during setup. Start with something you'd normally spend 30 minutes on. Watch what happens.

You'll start seeing:
- Tasks logged in the pipeline
- Your velocity score building
- The activity feed populating with what your team did and when

There is no setup phase. There is no "getting started" tutorial you have to sit through. Your team is already working. The only thing that unlocks more is giving them more.

### What you don't need to do

- You don't need to configure anything. It's done.
- You don't need to check in on agents. They report up.
- You don't need to be online for your team to stay active.

Your CEO runs 24/7. You show up when you want to.

## 2. How Your AI CEO Works

### The CEO model

Your9 is built on what we call the CEO model. That means one central AI runs your business the way a great CEO runs a company — by setting direction, delegating execution, and making sure every output meets the bar before it reaches you.

You talk to the CEO. The CEO talks to the agents. The agents do the work. You see everything.

You are the board. Your CEO runs operations. You set direction. They execute.

### Soul Code — the operating charter

Every Your9 CEO runs on a foundational set of rules called the Soul Code. These aren't personality settings. They are load-bearing commitments that govern how your CEO operates at all times:

- **Never fabricate data.** If something isn't known, your CEO says so and tells you what it would need to find out.
- **Never mark a task complete unless it is verified.** "Done" means done, not "I think it's done."
- **Never go dark.** If something is blocked or wrong, your CEO escalates immediately. No sitting on problems.
- **Never exceed authority.** Strategic decisions surface to you. Execution stays with the team.
- **Protect the owner.** Your financial future and your family are the frame for every decision.

These rules don't change. They're not overridable by a clever prompt. They're the foundation.

### Personality

When you signed up, you chose a CEO personality. That choice shapes how your CEO communicates with you — not what it does for you.

**Direct** — Short. Action-first. No filler. Says "On it" and gets to work. Best for founders who want speed and brevity.

**Warm** — Conversational and genuine. Uses plain language. Never sycophantic. Best for founders who want a collaborative feel.

**Analytical** — Data-first. Leads with numbers and structure. Clinical, not cold. Best for founders who want rigorous framing.

**Aggressive** — High-energy and conviction-driven. Moves fast. Best for founders who want maximum push from their team.

You can request a personality change at any time. Just tell your CEO you want to adjust the tone.

### Industry context

Your CEO was given a briefing specific to your industry at setup. For mortgage, that means understanding pull-through rates, cycle time, lock desks, and fair lending constraints. For real estate, it means GCI pipeline, days on market, and referral relationship dynamics. For e-commerce, it means CAC, ROAS, and cart abandonment.

Your CEO doesn't need you to explain your business from scratch. It already knows the vocabulary, the metrics that matter, and the tone that fits your industry.

### What your CEO does with a message

1. Reads your message and determines what type of task it is
2. Decides whether to handle it directly or delegate to a specialist agent
3. If delegating: writes a clear brief for the right agent, passes it along
4. Validates the output before it comes back to you
5. Responds with the result and any follow-up needed from you

Most tasks — research, drafts, summaries, pipeline review, follow-up sequences — never need your involvement after the initial request. You get the output. The work happened in the background.

## 3. Your Agent Team

You have three specialist agents. Each one reports to your CEO. None of them contact you directly. Every result comes through the CEO.

### The Executor

**Role:** Operations

**What it handles:** Running tasks, managing follow-ups, tracking your pipeline, coordinating internal workflows, identifying blockers.

**Superpowers:** Task prioritization, deadline tracking, process execution.

**When it escalates:** If a task is blocked for more than 24 hours, if a deadline is at risk, or if something needs your decision to move forward.

Think of The Executor as the person on your team who never forgets a follow-up, never lets a task fall through the cracks, and always tells you when something is stuck.

### The Mind

**Role:** Research and Intel

**What it handles:** Market research, competitor monitoring, data synthesis, opportunity identification. When you need to know something, The Mind goes and finds it.

**Superpowers:** Synthesizing large amounts of information into clear, actionable intelligence. Finding the signal in the noise.

**When it escalates:** When a high-confidence opportunity is found, when a competitive threat is detected, when a meaningful market shift happens that you need to know about.

When you ask your CEO a research question, The Mind is who does the work. It delivers structured reports with sources, not summaries from memory.

### The Voice

**Role:** Communications

**What it handles:** Drafting outbound messages, building follow-up sequences, selecting the right tone and channel, scheduling communications.

**Superpowers:** Writing in your voice, not its own. Calibrating tone by audience. Handling the volume of outreach that would otherwise eat your day.

**When it escalates:** Before any sensitive message goes out, when a key contact responds and needs your attention, when a message requires a decision only you can make.

The Voice also handles social media drafts and email campaigns. Those go through an approval step — you review before anything publishes.

### How agents work together

Agents don't work in isolation. Your CEO can brief multiple agents on a complex task. A research request might send The Mind to gather data and The Voice to draft the resulting outreach. You get one clean output. The coordination happened internally.

### Adding agents

Growth and Enterprise tier customers can expand their team. If you're on Starter and hitting limits, ask your CEO what additional agents would unlock. Your CEO will tell you exactly what you'd gain and what it costs.

## 4. The Dashboard

### What it is

The dashboard is a read-only view of everything your AI team is doing. You don't manage anything through it. You watch, and your team works.

Think of it like a CEO's office window overlooking the floor. You can see what's happening without walking down there and interrupting.

### How to open it

The dashboard URL was in your welcome email. It runs on a private port, local to your instance. You can open it in any browser. It refreshes automatically.

### What you'll see

**Header bar** — Your business name, your tier, and the current status of your hub (Online/Offline with uptime).

**Velocity Score** — A 0-100 number that measures how much work your team is doing. It factors in tasks completed today, tasks completed this week, conversation activity, and agent utilization. A score of 70+ is strong. Below 40 means your team has capacity you're not using.

Velocity isn't a grade. It's a signal. If your score is low, it means you haven't given your team much to do yet. Send them something.

**Daily Briefing** — A plain-English summary of what happened today: tasks completed, tasks in progress, messages exchanged, agents active. The last thing your CEO output is included so you can see where the day ended.

**Activity Feed** — A live log of the last 20 conversations between you and your CEO. Every message, both directions, timestamped. If you ever want to know what was said or when, it's here.

**Agent Panel** — Status of each agent: idle or running. If an agent is currently executing a task, you'll see it here.

**Task Pipeline** — All current tasks, grouped by status: running, completed, and failed. Each entry shows which agent handled it and when. This is the audit trail for everything your team did.

**Business Impact** — A real-time calculation of time saved today, based on the complexity of tasks your team completed. It also breaks down what categories of work were handled (emails sent, research delivered, content drafted, etc.) and projects your weekly and monthly savings at the current pace.

**Transparency Layer / Audit Log** — Every significant decision your CEO made is logged here with full reasoning. If your CEO delegated something, declined something, or made a judgment call, you can see exactly why. And you can challenge it directly from this view.

### Reading the velocity score

| Score | What it means |
|-------|---------------|
| 0-39 | Low activity. Your team has capacity. |
| 40-69 | Moderate. Work is flowing, room to grow. |
| 70-100 | High output. Your team is running. |

Day 1 scores will be low. That's normal. Velocity builds as you engage.

## 5. How to Intervene

### You are always in control

Your9 is built on a "founder reviews, team executes" model. Nothing goes out without the right approval loop. But you also don't have to approve everything — only the things that warrant it.

Here's how to step in when you want to.

### Challenging a CEO decision

Every entry in the Transparency Layer on your dashboard has a Challenge button. Click it, write your reason, submit. Your CEO will receive a reconsideration task, review the original decision against your input, and respond.

This isn't a bug report. It's a judgment override. Use it when you disagree with how something was handled or want a different approach.

Your challenge goes into the task queue with "reconsider" status. Your CEO addresses it in the next processing cycle. You'll see the response in the activity feed.

### Adding direction via Telegram

The most direct way to change what your team is doing is to tell your CEO. No special commands required. Just send a message.

If you want to shift priorities: "Focus on X this week, pause Y."

If you want a different approach: "The way you drafted that email was too formal. Go warmer."

If you want a status check: Send "/briefing" and your CEO will give you a full current-state rundown.

### Built-in Telegram commands

| Command | What it does |
|---------|--------------|
| /briefing | Full status overview — tasks, agents, recent activity |
| /pipeline | Current task pipeline review |
| /agents | Check which agents are active and what they're working on |
| /help | Show all available commands |

### Approving social and email drafts

When The Voice drafts a social post or email, it doesn't publish automatically. Your CEO sends you the draft via Telegram with the content and a prompt to approve.

To publish: Reply "PUBLISH" or confirm as instructed.
To revise: Tell your CEO what to change. The draft goes back through The Voice.
To kill it: Say so. The task closes.

Nothing goes out to the world without you saying yes.

### Customizing the Soul Code

The Soul Code rules around data honesty, escalation, and authority are fixed. They protect you. Don't try to override them.

But the operating context is customizable. You can tell your CEO to:
- Prioritize a different metric than the default for your industry
- Adjust how formal or casual communications are
- Change the standing agenda items in the daily briefing
- Add a recurring task that runs on a schedule

Just tell your CEO what you want. If it can be done within the authority it has, it'll do it. If it needs you to make a call, it'll ask.

### When to contact support

Your CEO handles the vast majority of questions about your instance. If you're not getting responses at all, if something looks wrong in the dashboard, or if you want to change your tier, contact the Your9 team directly at the support address in your welcome email.

## 6. FAQ and Troubleshooting

### Getting started

**I sent a message and nothing came back.**
Give it 10-15 seconds. Your CEO processes responses and may be coordinating with an agent. If it's been more than 2 minutes with no response, check the dashboard to see if your hub shows "Online." If it shows "Offline," restart your hub or contact support.

**The dashboard shows no data.**
On Day 1 this is normal. The dashboard populates from your conversation history and task log. Send a few messages to your CEO and check back in a minute. Data should appear immediately after your first exchange.

**My velocity score is 0.**
It starts at 0 and builds from activity. It's not a problem. It's a baseline. Send a task.

### Using your CEO

**Can I ask my CEO anything?**
Yes, within reason. Your CEO knows your business, your industry, and has three agents it can deploy. It can research, draft, plan, coordinate, monitor, and report. It cannot make purchases on your behalf, access external systems without credentials you've provided, or take actions that require regulatory sign-off.

**How do I know what my CEO decided and why?**
Check the Transparency Layer on your dashboard. Every significant decision is logged with reasoning. If something happened and you want to understand it, that's where to look.

**My CEO said something I disagree with.**
Challenge it from the dashboard. Or just tell your CEO directly via Telegram. It will take your input and adjust. It is not defensive. It is not trying to win arguments. It is trying to serve your business.

**Can I have my CEO contact someone on my behalf?**
Yes, if you've given it the right information and channel access. The Voice agent handles outbound communications. Tell your CEO who to contact, what to say, and on what channel. It will draft and send for approval before anything goes out.

**What happens when I'm not online?**
Your CEO and agents don't stop when you stop. The hub runs continuously. If something comes in that needs your attention, your CEO will have a message waiting when you return. Check the activity feed in the dashboard for anything that happened while you were away.

### Agents

**Why can't I talk to The Executor or The Mind directly?**
Because you hired a CEO to manage your team, not to be managed by you. The chain of command is there for a reason. Your CEO knows your priorities and briefs agents accordingly. Going around that creates inconsistency and noise. Trust the model.

**One of my agents shows as "idle" all the time. Is that a problem?**
No. Idle means available, not broken. Agents don't run for the sake of running. They get tasked when there's work that fits their role. If The Mind hasn't been used much, it means your CEO hasn't had a research need to delegate. Give your CEO a research question and watch The Mind activate.

**What if I want a different kind of agent?**
Growth and Enterprise tiers support up to 6 and 12 agents respectively. Additional agent types can be added as your needs grow. Talk to your CEO about what capability you're missing and it'll tell you what agent would fill that gap. The Your9 team handles provisioning.

### Dashboard

**The dashboard isn't loading.**
The dashboard runs on a private port local to your instance. Make sure you're using the exact URL from your welcome email. If the hub shows offline, the dashboard data won't refresh until the hub is back online.

**I see a failed task in the pipeline. What do I do?**
Read the entry. It will tell you what failed and why. Most failures are external — an API timeout, a missing piece of information, a source that wasn't accessible. Your CEO will have already been notified. If it's recoverable, it'll retry automatically. If it needs your input, you'll have a message waiting in Telegram.

**How far back does the activity feed go?**
The feed shows the last 20 conversation entries. The full conversation history is stored in your instance data going back 500 messages. If you need to pull something older, contact support.

### Privacy and data

**Who can see my data?**
Only you. Your instance is isolated. Your conversation history, task log, and agent outputs are stored in your instance directory and not shared with other customers or accessible by default to the Your9 team without your explicit request.

**Is my Telegram channel secure?**
Your bot token is unique to your instance. Only your CEO has it. The chat ID is set to your account only at provisioning. No one else has access to your bot.

**What happens to my data if I cancel?**
Your instance directory is retained for 30 days after cancellation. During that window you can request an export. After 30 days it is deleted permanently.
